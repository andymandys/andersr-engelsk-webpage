State symbols

Hawaii's state flower is the yellow hibiscus. As the name suggests, it is yellow and very bright like the sun. It represents beauty and happiness. The state bird is the nene. However it is also known as the Hawaiian goose, and it is the rarest goose in the world, only found in Hawaii. The marine animal is the humpback whale. Every year, thousands of humpback whales migrate to Hwaii's watm water to breed and reproduce. Their motto is "Ua mau ke ea o ka 'aina i ka pono" which means " The life of the land is perpetuated in righteousness" These are most of the state symbols of Hawaii, and shows Hawaii's natural beauty and culture.

Political system

Hawaii, as you probably know, is one of the 50 states of America. It became the 50th state on August 21, 1959. The state has a democratic political system with three branches of government. The executive, legislative and judicial branch. The govener is the head of the executive branch. The legisLative branch consists of the Hawaii State senate and the Hawaii House of Representetives. And lastly the judicial branch is made up of the Hawaii State supreme court and Lower courts. The executive branch enforces the laws, the LegisLative branch makes the Laws and the judicial branch interprets the laws. The state government is modeled after the federal government of the United States.

Historical Places

Hawaii is home to many different historical and cultural places. The lolani palace in Honolulu for example. This palace was once the royale home to the Hawaiian monarchy. It is the only royal palace in the United States. Another historical place is Pearl Harbor. This place is known for the attack on Pearl Harbor by the Japanese on December 7, 1941. This attack led to the United States entering world war 2. Today you can visit the USS Arizona memorial at Pearl Harbor. And lastly we have the Pu'uhonua o Honaunau national historical park. This place was once a place of refuge for ancient Hawaiians who broke the law. However today it is a place where you can learn about Hawaiian culture and history. There are many more historical places in Hawaii, but these are some of the most famous ones. Fun facts

There are many fun facts to learn about Hawaii, but here are a few. Did you know that Hawaii is the only state in the US that grows coffee comercialy? The coffee is called Kona coffee and is grown on the big island. Surfing was actually invented in Hawaii. The ancient Hawaiians called it "He'e nalu" which means wave sliding in english. A cool fact that is Hawaii is one of the four states that have banned billboards. This is in order to preserve the natural beauty of the islands. Hawaii is the only state that cononsists entirely of islands. In total there are 137 island in Hawaii. Probably the most fun fact about hawaii is that it is known as the Rainbow state.
